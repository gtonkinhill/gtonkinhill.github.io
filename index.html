Gerry Tonkin-Hill

PhD student

I'm an applied statistician mainly working on infectious disease. I am completing a PhD under the supervision of Stephen Bentley, Simon Frost and Jukka Corander at the Wellcome Sanger Institute and Cambridge University. My PhD project is focused on applying statistical techniques to investigate within-host diversity and transmission in Streptococcus pneumoniae. Previously I worked on Malaria with Tony Papenfuss, Michael Duffy and Karen Day investigating the major virulence factor Pfemp1 in Plasmodium falciparum.
